What is Zoho?

Zoho is an Indian multinational technology company that provides cloud-based software solutions for businesses, including CRM, accounting, project management, and office productivity tools.

When was Zoho founded, and who is its founder?

Zoho was founded in 1996 by Sridhar Vembu and Tony Thomas.

What was Zoho's original name before rebranding?

Zoho was originally called AdventNet Inc. before rebranding to Zoho Corporation in 2009.

What is Zoho One, and why is it significant?

Zoho One is an all-in-one suite that includes over 45 cloud-based applications for business operations, offering seamless integration and cost-effectiveness.

Which cloud-based CRM software is offered by Zoho?

Zoho CRM is the company's cloud-based customer relationship management software, helping businesses manage sales, marketing, and customer support.

What is Zoho Creator used for?

Zoho Creator is a low-code development platform that allows users to build custom applications without extensive programming knowledge.

How does Zoho differentiate itself from competitors like Salesforce and Microsoft?

Zoho offers cost-effective, privacy-focused, and deeply integrated solutions with no dependency on third-party advertising or data monetization.

What initiative did Zoho launch to promote rural development in India?

Zoho launched the "Rural Revival" initiative, setting up offices in rural areas to provide job opportunities and reduce migration to urban centers.

Which AI-powered assistant does Zoho offer in its software suite?

Zoho offers Zia, an AI-powered assistant that provides predictive analytics, automation, and business insights across its applications.

What major data privacy stance does Zoho take compared to other SaaS companies?

Zoho strictly avoids ad-based revenue models, ensuring user data privacy by not selling or sharing customer information with third parties.

What is the employee count of Zoho as of 2024?

As of 2024, Zoho has over 20,000 employees globally.
